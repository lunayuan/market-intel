# Executive Summary

The exec summary is the "if you read nothing else" section. Write it AFTER all category sections are complete.

## Rules

- **3-5 bullet points.** No more. No less than 3 unless it's genuinely a dead week.
- **Lead with the single most important item.** The one that most directly affects `config.company.decisions`.
- **Each bullet is ONE sentence.** Not a paragraph. Not two sentences. One.
- **Prioritize items with direct company implications** over general market noise.
- **Bold the key entity or event** in each bullet.
- **No filler.** No "This week saw several developments in..." — just the signals.

## Example

```
- **Honda cancels 3 North American EV models** — major strategy reassessment signals shifting OEM commitment to pure-EV timelines
- **Aptiv spinning off its Electrical Distribution Systems business** — direct competitor restructuring creates potential market opportunities
- **Euro 7 standards arrive November 2026** — new brake particle and cybersecurity requirements will drive connector spec changes
```

## "What to Watch Next Week"

After the main category sections (not inside the exec summary), include 2-4 forward-looking items:
- Upcoming regulation deadlines
- Anticipated OEM/competitor announcements
- Industry conferences or events
- Earnings calls from competitors or key customers
- Trade policy decisions

Format as a simple bullet list under the heading "WHAT TO WATCH NEXT WEEK."
